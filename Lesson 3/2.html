The Eiffel Tower, an iconic symbol of Paris, is a wrought-iron lattice tower located on the Champ de Mars. Completed in 1889 by engineer Gustave Eiffel for the World's Fair, it stands 330 meters (1,083 feet) tall and was the tallest structure in the world at the time. Initially criticized by some for its bold design, the tower has since become a global cultural icon, attracting millions of visitors annually. Its breathtaking views, intricate structure, and shimmering lights at night make it a must-see destination.
